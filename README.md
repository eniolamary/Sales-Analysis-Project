# Sales Data Analysis Project (SQL + Power BI)
### Overview
This project explores sales performance, customer behavior, product trends, and regional insights using SQL for data analysis and Power BI for interactive visualization.

### The workflow involved:

- Data cleaning and transformation using SQL
- Exploratory data analysis using SQL queries
- Building an interactive Power BI dashboard for visualization and business insights

### Tools Used
- SQL (data cleaning + analysis)
- Power BI (dashboard & visualization)
- Excel / dataset source

## SQL Work
SQL was used to:

- Clean and prepare raw sales data
- Aggregate revenue, ratings, and customer segments
- Identify top-performing products, regions, and customer groups
- Analyze time-based trends (monthly, quarterly, weekday patterns)
  
## Key Insights from Analysis (bullet form for scanning)
- Male customers aged 31–35 purchasing Clothing generated the highest revenue
- The “Other” product category had the highest ratings but lowest sales volume, indicating untapped opportunity
- Fridays consistently recorded the highest daily sales performance
- Sydney was the top-performing region
- Q2 recorded the highest revenue, though overall seasonal variation was minimal

## Detailed Analysis 
- Male customers purchasing Clothing products generated the highest total revenue, contributing over ₦447K in sales. In contrast, female customers purchasing products in the ‘Other’ category recorded the lowest sales performance. This suggests stronger engagement and purchasing activity among male fashion buyers, while the ‘Other’ category may require better product positioning or targeted marketing for female customers.
- The ‘Other’ product category recorded the highest average customer rating despite generating the lowest overall sales volume. This suggests strong customer satisfaction but limited product visibility or market reach, presenting a potential growth opportunity. In contrast, the Clothing category remained the strongest revenue-generating segment, despite maintaining a more moderate average rating of 3.5. Most Clothing customers also belonged to the high-value customer segment, indicating strong purchasing activity and customer retention within this category.
- Sales performance remained relatively stable across all quarters, with Q2 generating the highest overall revenue by a narrow margin of approximately ₦10K. This indicates consistent demand throughout the year rather than strong seasonal fluctuations. At the monthly level, May 2024 recorded the highest sales performance, while Fridays consistently generated the strongest daily sales activity, likely driven by increased customer spending ahead of the weekend. Geographically, Sydney emerged as the top-performing location in overall revenue contribution.

## Business Recommendations
- Increase visibility and marketing for high-rated low-sales products
- Focus on high-value male clothing customers (31–35 segment)
- Run major promotions on Fridays to maximize sales performance
- Replicate successful strategies from Sydney in lower-performing regions
- Maintain stable inventory planning due to consistent demand across quarters
